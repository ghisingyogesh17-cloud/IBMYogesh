Dashrath Chand

Dasharath Chand Thakuri (1903 – 28 January 1941) was a martyr of the Nepalese Democratic Movement and a crucial politician of Nepal who was highly active in starting a political revolution in Nepal during the Rana rule. He was born at Baskot of Baitadi district of Nepal in 1903 AD to Sher Bahadur Chand. Dasharathchand, a municipality in Baitadi, is named in honor of his legacy.

Education

He received his education at different places of Kumaon like Almora, Nainital, and other regions. Finally, he went to Banaras, where he completed his Intermediate level education. While in Banaras, he also dedicated his efforts working for the Kashi Nagari Pracharini Sabha.

Political Career and Death

During his student days, Indian citizens were actively trying to free India from British rule. He was deeply impressed by this freedom movement in India and became actively involved. At the same time, he remembered the miserable condition of the general public back in Nepal, who were suffering from extreme poverty, illiteracy, and ignorance. The Nepalese public was being severely exploited by the ruling class, who lived in extreme luxury and obeyed no law. Tyranny, debauchery, economic exploitation, and religious persecution characterized the Rana rule, which is widely recognized as one of the darkest periods of Nepalese history.

Dasharath Chand could not resist protesting against these rulers and became determined to bring an end to the autocratic regime. He began writing powerful articles against the Ranas in the newspaper Janata, published from Patna in India, under the pen name "Sewasingh".

Later, Dasharath Chand met Tanka Prasad Acharya and Dharma Bhakta Mathema, who were also seeking to spark a political revolution in Nepal. A political party called the Nepal Praja Parishad was soon organized under the presidency of Tanka Prasad Acharya, with the active participation and leadership of Dasharath Chand. The group started secretly distributing pamphlets exposing the arbitrary nature of the Rana administration.

For about four months, the government remained completely unaware of who was behind the campaign. Eventually, the regime announced a large reward of Rs. 5,000 for information. Tempted by this reward, a member of the group, Ramji Joshi, disclosed the secret, leading to widespread arrests. Dasharath Chand and Gangalal Shrestha were captured and subjected to severe torture in prison. Despite being pressured to beg for forgiveness to save their lives, both refused to yield. On 28 January 1941, Dasharath Chand and Gangalal were shot dead at Shobha Bhagawati.
